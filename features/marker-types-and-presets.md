# Marker Types & Presets

Every shoot is different. A podcast needs different markers than a sports event. That's why every marker button in Marker is fully customizable - colors, names, and presets you can switch with one tap.

## Marker Buttons

You have **four marker buttons** on your recording screen. Each button has:

- A **color** that matches your editing software's marker colors
- A **name** that appears as the marker label in your timeline

### Available Colors

Colors depend on your selected editing software:

**DaVinci Resolve** - 16 colors:
Red, Blue, Green, Yellow, Cyan, Pink, Purple, Fuchsia, Rose, Lavender, Sky, Mint, Lemon, Sand, Cocoa, Cream

**Premiere Pro** - 7 colors:
Green, Red, Purple, Orange, Yellow, Blue, Turquoise

**Final Cut Pro** - 4 styles:
Enchanted, Lavender, Midnights, Lilac

::: info
Final Cut Pro doesn't natively support marker colors the way DaVinci Resolve and Premiere Pro do. Marker uses four distinct visual styles so you can still differentiate your markers.
:::

### Customizing a Button

Go to **Settings > Marker Presets** and tap any of the four marker rows. You can change the **name** and **color** of each button.

When you change a button's color, the name automatically updates to match - unless you've given it a custom name.

## Marker Presets

Think of presets as quick profiles for your marker buttons. Different shoots need different marker setups.

For a **podcast**, you might want:
- Button 1: Red - "Cut"
- Button 2: Green - "Highlight"
- Button 3: Blue - "Topic Switch"
- Button 4: Yellow - "Reel Moment"

For a **sports event**, you might want:
- Button 1: Red - "Goal"
- Button 2: Green - "Action"
- Button 3: Blue - "Replay"
- Button 4: Yellow - "Crowd Reaction"

### Creating and Managing Presets

In **Settings > Marker Presets**, you'll see a horizontal row of preset buttons at the top:

- **Tap a preset** to switch your active markers to that setup
- **Tap +** to create a new preset
- **Long-press a preset** to rename, duplicate, or delete it

You can have up to 5 presets per editing software. Presets are separate for DaVinci Resolve, Premiere Pro, and Final Cut Pro - so you can have custom setups for each.

### Timeline Presets

Each marker preset also includes **timeline presets** - pre-defined timeline names that automatically appear when you start a new session. If you always create the same timelines for your podcast (e.g., "Full Episode", "Highlights", "Shorts"), set them up once in the preset and they'll be ready every time.

→ [Learn about Timelines](/features/timelines-and-clips)

## In/Out Markers

Instead of marking a single point, you can mark a range:

1. **Press and hold** any marker button
2. The in-point is set when you press down
3. The out-point is set when you release
4. After 1.5 seconds of holding, a timer appears showing the duration

In/Out markers are perfect for:
- Marking a segment to cut out
- Highlighting a complete take
- Defining a section for a reel or short
- Marking an entire answer in an interview

In your editing timeline, In/Out markers appear as a range (with both in and out timecodes) so you can immediately see the marked segment.
