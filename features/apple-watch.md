# Apple Watch

Your iPhone might be in your pocket, clipped to a rig, or sitting on a table across the room. Your Apple Watch is always on your wrist.

That's what makes it perfect for setting markers.

## What You Can Do

Once connected, your Apple Watch shows:

- **Four marker buttons** matching your current iPhone preset (same colors and names)
- **Session timer** showing elapsed time
- **Last marker** name and time for quick reference

Tap any button to set a marker. It syncs to your iPhone instantly.

## Setting Up

1. Make sure your Apple Watch and iPhone are paired normally through the Watch app
2. Open Marker on your iPhone
3. Open the Marker app on your Apple Watch - it appears automatically when the iPhone app is installed
4. Start a session on your iPhone
5. Your Apple Watch shows the marker buttons

The Watch app mirrors your iPhone's current marker preset. If you change presets on the iPhone, the Watch updates automatically.

::: tip
The Apple Watch is especially useful when your iPhone is mounted on a camera rig, tripod, or gimbal where you can't easily reach the screen. One tap on your wrist and the marker is set.
:::

## On Set

The Apple Watch works best for simple, fast marking:

- **Podcasts:** Your hands are free and the Watch is silent - no tapping sounds picked up by microphones
- **Sports:** Tap your wrist without looking away from the action
- **Events:** Mark highlights discreetly while managing the shoot
- **Solo filmmakers:** Set markers while operating the camera with both hands

The marker appears immediately on your iPhone's history list. Notes can be added later from the iPhone or in the Companion App during editing.
