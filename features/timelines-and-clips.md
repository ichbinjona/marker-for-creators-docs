# Timelines & Clips

A 3-hour conference. A full day of event coverage. A 90-minute interview with 6 topic changes. Without timelines, your editor has to scrub through the entire recording to find each section. With timelines, every segment is already separated before they even open the project.

## What Are Timelines?

Timelines let you split your recording into named segments - without stopping your session. Think of them like folders for your markers.

When you create a timeline called "Keynote Speech" and activate it during the keynote, all markers you set during that period are automatically tagged with that timeline. In your editing software, each timeline becomes a separate sequence or project with only the relevant footage.

## Timeline Modes

You have three modes, configurable in **Settings > Advanced > Timeline Mode**:

### Single Timeline

One timeline is active at a time. Tap a timeline to activate it - the previously active one automatically deactivates.

**Best for:** Content with clear, sequential sections. A conference with separate talks. A podcast with distinct segments.

### Multi Timeline

Multiple timelines can be active at the same time. Tapping a timeline toggles it on or off without affecting others.

**Best for:** Overlapping categories. For example, filming a concert where you want to track both "Wide Shots" and "Song 3" simultaneously - a clip can belong to both timelines.

### Tagger Mode

Tap a timeline to "tag" the current moment. The timeline activates for a brief instant and deactivates automatically. A "TAGGED" badge confirms the tag.

**Best for:** B-roll tagging. Filming a documentary and you spot a great landscape shot - tap the "Scenery" timeline to tag it. You don't need to activate and deactivate manually.

## Using Timelines During Recording

Swipe to the **third page** (right) of the recording screen to see your timelines.

- **Default Timeline** always appears at the top - it represents all clips from the entire session
- Your custom timelines appear below
- Tap any timeline to activate/deactivate/tag it (depending on your mode)
- Active timelines show a colored border

### Creating Timelines

Three ways to create a timeline:

1. **Note Bar:** Type a name in the text field at the bottom and tap the checkmark (or press Return)
2. **Plus button:** Tap + to create a timeline with an auto-generated name
3. **Presets:** Configure timeline presets so they're created automatically when you start a session

### Managing Timelines

- **Rename:** Long-press a timeline card and select "Rename"
- **Delete:** Swipe left on a timeline card, or long-press and select "Delete"

## Timeline Presets

If you film the same type of content regularly, set up timeline presets so your timelines are ready every session.

In **Settings > Marker Presets**, select a preset and scroll to **Timelines**. Add the timeline names you always need. When you start a session, these timelines are created automatically.

Example for a podcast with multiple segments:
- "Full Episode"
- "Highlights"
- "Shorts"

Example for a multi-day event:
- "Stage A"
- "Stage B"
- "Backstage"
- "Crowd"

## Minimum Timeline Duration

Short accidental activations can clutter your timelines. In **Settings > Advanced > Min. Duration**, set a minimum length (0s, 1s, 2s, or 3s). When a session ends, any timeline activation shorter than this threshold is automatically discarded.

Tagger Mode is exempt from this - tags are always kept regardless of their duration.

## Timelines in Your Editor

Each timeline becomes a separate sequence in your editing software with only the footage from the periods when that timeline was active. Your markers are placed at the correct positions within each sequence.

The Default Timeline contains everything - your complete session as one continuous sequence.

::: info
Timelines require the Companion App for import. They are not available in Legacy Export mode.
:::
