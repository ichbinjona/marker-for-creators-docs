# Event Highlights Workflow

8 hours of footage. 90 seconds of highlights due by tomorrow morning. That's the math of event videography - and without a system, you're spending tonight scrubbing through endless footage at 2x speed, desperately trying to remember where the good stuff happened.

With Marker, you already know.

## Before the Event

### Set Up Your Markers

Create a marker preset called "Event":

| Button | Color | Name | Use |
|--------|-------|------|-----|
| 1 | Red | Hero Shot | The definitive moment - keynote applause, award handoff, group photo |
| 2 | Green | Highlight | Great moment worth including in the reel |
| 3 | Blue | B-Roll | Beautiful establishing shots, detail shots, atmosphere |
| 4 | Yellow | Reaction | Laughs, cheers, emotional responses from attendees |

### Set Up Your Timelines

Events have a natural structure. Use it:

- **Ceremony** or **Main Stage**
- **Party / Reception**
- **Speeches / Toasts**
- **B-Roll**
- **Reel** - Best moments across all categories

### Set Up Your Camera

**Time of Day** timecode. If you're running multiple cameras (one on stage, one roaming), sync both to the same clock. This is critical for multi-cam matching in the Companion.

## During the Event

Events are long and unpredictable. You can't focus on your phone the whole time - you're filming, moving, adjusting settings. The Apple Watch makes this manageable.

1. Start a **Time of Day** session at the beginning of the event
2. Film and mark simultaneously:

| When | Tap |
|------|-----|
| The big moment everyone will remember | **Hero Shot** (Red) |
| A great candid moment, toast, or performance | **Highlight** (Green) |
| Beautiful wide shot, detail, decor, or location | **B-Roll** (Blue) |
| Audience laughing, clapping, or reacting emotionally | **Reaction** (Yellow) |

3. Switch timelines as the event progresses. Activate **Ceremony** during the ceremony, **Party** during the party, **B-Roll** when you're getting establishing shots.
4. Whenever you have a free moment (between segments, during break), add notes to recent markers: "CEO opens keynote" or "Sunset over venue"

::: tip
For an 8-hour event, aim for 40-80 markers. That might sound like a lot, but spread over 8 hours it's about one every 6-12 minutes. You'll thank yourself during the edit.
:::

## After the Event

1. Import your footage (SD card or transfer to your editing machine)
2. Open the **Companion App** - sessions sync and footage matches
3. Review markers and add any missing notes
4. Click **Play**

## Building the Highlight Reel

This is where the time savings are massive.

Your editor opens the project. Instead of scrubbing through 8 hours:

1. **Hero Shot markers** (Red) - These are your anchor moments. Start here. The reel opens and closes with hero moments.
2. **Highlight markers** (Green) - Fill in between hero moments with the best of the event.
3. **B-Roll markers** (Blue) - Use these as transitions, establishing shots, and breathing room between highlights.
4. **Reaction markers** (Yellow) - Layer these over other clips for emotional impact.

The Reel timeline gives you a pre-filtered sequence with only the moments you flagged as reel-worthy. Your editor literally just trims and arranges - no searching.

## Time Saved

Without markers: 4-8 hours of review + 2-4 hours of editing = next-day delivery at best.

With markers: Skip the review entirely. 1-2 hours of editing. Reel delivered the same night.

For clients who expect fast turnaround - same-day previews, next-morning delivery - Marker is the difference between pulling an all-nighter and going to bed at a reasonable hour.
