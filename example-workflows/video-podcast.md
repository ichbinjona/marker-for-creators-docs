# Video Podcast Workflow

You're filming a podcast. Two cameras, maybe three. The conversation runs 90 minutes to 3 hours. Your editor wasn't in the room - they have no idea what happened, when the energy was high, or where the topics changed.

That's where Marker changes everything.

## Before Recording

### Set Up Your Markers

Create a marker preset called "Podcast":

| Button | Color | Name | Use |
|--------|-------|------|-----|
| 1 | Red | Cut | Mark a section to remove (awkward pause, phone ringing, re-take) |
| 2 | Green | Highlight | The guest said something great - this goes in the highlights reel |
| 3 | Blue | Topic | New topic or segment starts here |
| 4 | Yellow | Reel | This moment works for a social media clip |

### Set Up Your Timelines

Add these timeline presets:

- **Full Episode** - Keep this active the entire time. This is your main edit.
- **Highlights** - Activate when something reel-worthy happens.
- **Shorts** - Activate when a moment works as a standalone clip.

### Set Up Your Camera

Both cameras should be on **Time of Day** timecode. Verify the clock on each camera matches your phone.

## During Recording

1. Start a **Time of Day** session in Marker
2. Place your iPhone where you can reach it - on the table, clipped to the host's chair, or on a small stand. Alternatively, use the **Apple Watch** so your hands stay free and there's no tapping sound near the microphones.
3. Set markers as the conversation flows:

| When | Tap |
|------|-----|
| Guest starts a new answer worth keeping | **Highlight** (Green) |
| Topic changes to a new subject | **Topic** (Blue) |
| Something quotable, funny, or emotional happens | **Reel** (Yellow) |
| Technical issue, long pause, "let me start over" | **Cut** (Red) |

4. Use **note-to-next-marker** for topic labels: Type "Guest: origin story" in the Note Bar, tap →, then tap Topic when the segment begins.
5. Switch timelines: Activate **Highlights** during great segments. Activate **Shorts** when a self-contained moment happens. Switch back to just **Full Episode** during normal conversation.

::: tip
Most podcast shoots generate 10-30 markers per hour. You don't need to mark everything - just the moments that matter.
:::

## After Recording

1. Open the **Companion App** - your session syncs automatically
2. Review your markers. Add notes to any markers you missed: "Guest talks about their first failure" or "Skip - phone rang"
3. Click **Play** - your editing software opens with:
   - All camera angles on the timeline
   - Markers at every point you marked
   - Separate sequences for Full Episode, Highlights, and Shorts

## In the Edit

Your editor opens the project and immediately knows:
- Red markers = skip or cut around
- Green markers = the good stuff
- Blue markers = segment boundaries
- Yellow markers = social media clips

For the full episode, they work through the Timeline with the Topic markers as chapter boundaries. For the highlights reel, they open the Highlights timeline - only the best moments are there, already separated. For social media, the Shorts timeline has self-contained clips ready to trim and post.

## Time Saved

A typical 2-hour podcast generates 4-6 hours of editing without markers. With markers, the same edit takes 1-2 hours - because your editor never has to watch the full recording. They jump from marker to marker, knowing exactly what happened at each point.
