# Conference / Talk Recording Workflow

Five presentations, three panel discussions, eight hours of footage. Two cameras running the whole time. Tomorrow, each speaker needs their individual talk exported as a separate video. The conference organizer wants a sizzle reel by end of week.

Nobody is going to fast-forward through 8 hours to find where each talk starts and ends. That's what markers are for.

## Before the Conference

### Set Up Your Markers

Create a marker preset called "Conference":

| Button | Color | Name | Use |
|--------|-------|------|-----|
| 1 | Red | Talk Start | A new presentation or panel begins |
| 2 | Green | Key Point | Speaker says something quotable or insightful |
| 3 | Blue | Slide Change | Important slide transition (useful for edit points) |
| 4 | Yellow | Audience | Q&A moment, applause, audience reaction |

### Set Up Your Timelines

Create one timeline per talk or segment:

- **Keynote - Speaker Name**
- **Panel - Topic**
- **Workshop - Topic**
- **Networking / Breaks**
- **Sizzle Reel**

If the schedule has 8 sessions, create 8 timelines. Timeline presets make this fast - set them up once and they're ready when you start recording.

### Set Up Your Cameras

Both cameras on **Time of Day** timecode. Wide camera on stage, close-up camera on the speaker. Verify timecodes match.

## During the Conference

Conferences are a marathon. You're running cameras for hours. The Apple Watch is ideal here - you can mark without leaving your camera position.

1. Start a **Time of Day** session at the beginning of the day
2. Activate the correct timeline for each segment:

| When | Do |
|------|----|
| New speaker takes the stage | Deactivate previous timeline, activate new one. Tap **Talk Start** (Red) + note: "Dr. Smith - AI in Healthcare" |
| Speaker makes a key point | Tap **Key Point** (Green) |
| Important slide shown | Tap **Slide Change** (Blue) + note if relevant: "Revenue chart 2024" |
| Audience asks a question | Tap **Audience** (Yellow) |
| Break starts | Deactivate all timelines (or activate **Networking**) |
| Great moment for highlight reel | Also activate **Sizzle Reel** timeline briefly |

3. Between sessions, review your markers and add notes: "Closing joke - good for reel" or "Technical issues 2 min - skip"

::: tip
For talk recordings, **Talk Start** markers with notes containing the speaker name and topic are the most valuable. They turn 8 continuous hours into clearly separated segments.
:::

## After the Conference

1. Import all footage from both cameras
2. Open the **Companion App** - sessions sync, footage matches, multi-cam is detected
3. Review and add any missing notes
4. Click **Play**

## Delivering Individual Talks

Each timeline becomes a separate sequence in your editing software. For "Keynote - Dr. Smith":

1. Open that timeline's sequence
2. Both camera angles are there, markers at key moments
3. Trim the head and tail
4. Cut between wide and close-up angles at Key Point and Slide Change markers
5. Export

Repeat for each talk. What would take a full day of scrubbing through 8 hours of footage takes an afternoon - because each talk is already isolated in its own timeline.

## Building the Sizzle Reel

The Sizzle Reel timeline has every moment you flagged as reel-worthy across the entire conference. Your editor opens it and has a curated selection of the best 5-10 minutes from 8 hours. Trim, arrange, add music, done.

## Multi-Day Conferences

For multi-day events:

- Start a new session each day (or keep one running - sessions that cross midnight are handled automatically)
- The Companion splits multi-day sessions into Day 1, Day 2, etc.
- Timeline presets can be reused across days
- All days appear in the same editing project with correctly aligned timecodes

## Time Saved

A typical full-day conference with 6-8 talks:

- **Without markers:** 8-16 hours of footage review + 4-8 hours per talk for isolation and editing
- **With markers:** No footage review. 30-60 minutes per talk for final polish. Sizzle reel assembled in 2-3 hours from pre-marked highlights.

For regular conference videography, this is the difference between a week-long post-production and a 2-day turnaround.
