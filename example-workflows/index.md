# Example Workflows

Every type of production has its own rhythm. A podcast is different from a sports event, and both are different from a conference. These workflows show you how real creators use Marker for their specific content - so you can see exactly how it fits into yours.

## Choose Your Content Type

<div class="workflow-grid">

### [Video Podcast](/example-workflows/video-podcast)
You're filming 2-3 hours of conversation. Your markers tell the editor exactly where to cut, which moments to highlight, and where each topic starts and ends.

### [Sports Videography](/example-workflows/sports-videography)
Goals, saves, celebrations - you need to mark them in real-time because you can't ask the players to do it again. Same-day turnaround means every second of editing time matters.

### [Event Highlights](/example-workflows/event-highlights)
8 hours of footage, 90-second highlight reel due by tomorrow morning. Without markers, you're scrubbing through everything. With markers, you're editing in an hour.

### [Documentary / Reportage](/example-workflows/documentary)
Interviews, B-roll, location shoots across days or weeks. Notes on each marker turn your footage archive into a searchable library.

### [Conference / Talk Recording](/example-workflows/conference)
5 presentations, 3 panel discussions, 8 hours of multi-camera coverage. Nobody is going to fast-forward through all of it to find the key moments - unless you marked them.
