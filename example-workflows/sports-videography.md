# Sports Videography Workflow

The goalkeeper makes a save. The crowd erupts. You can't pause and rewind - the next play is already happening. In sports, you either mark the moment when it happens or you spend hours finding it later.

And in sports, turnaround time is everything. The highlight reel is due tonight.

## Before the Event

### Set Up Your Markers

Create a marker preset called "Sports":

| Button | Color | Name | Use |
|--------|-------|------|-----|
| 1 | Red | Goal / Score | A team scores or a decisive play happens |
| 2 | Green | Action | High-energy play, near-miss, great move |
| 3 | Blue | Replay | Worth a slow-motion replay in the edit |
| 4 | Yellow | Crowd / Emotion | Celebration, reaction, emotional moment |

Adjust the names for your sport. For basketball: Score, Block, Dunk, Timeout. For motorsports: Pass, Crash, Leader Change, Pit Stop.

### Set Up Your Camera

Set your camera to **Time of Day** timecode. If you're using multiple cameras (wide angle + close-up), make sure both are synced to the same clock.

### Set Up Your Timelines

If you're covering a longer event:

- **Full Game** - Everything
- **First Half / Second Half** - Split the coverage
- **Best Of** - Only the absolute highlights

## During the Event

The Apple Watch is your best friend here. Strap it on, keep your eyes on the action, and tap your wrist when something happens.

1. Start a **Time of Day** session
2. Focus on filming. When something happens:

| When | Tap |
|------|-----|
| A goal, score, or winning point | **Goal** (Red) |
| Great play, fast break, impressive move | **Action** (Green) |
| Worth seeing again in slow motion | **Replay** (Blue) |
| Crowd goes wild, player celebrates, emotional moment | **Crowd** (Yellow) |

3. Don't worry about notes during the game - speed matters more. You can add notes later.
4. Between halves or during breaks, swipe to your marker history and add notes to key markers: "Equalizer goal from free kick" or "Red card at 73 min"

::: tip
In fast sports, aim for 15-40 markers per hour. Tap quickly and keep filming. The goal is to narrow down your footage review, not to annotate everything perfectly.
:::

## After the Event

1. Connect your camera's SD card or import your footage
2. Open the **Companion App** - your session and footage match automatically
3. Add notes to any markers that need context
4. Click **Play** - your editor opens with the full timeline and all markers in place

## In the Edit

Your editor opens the project and can jump directly to every goal, every highlight, every crowd reaction. For a same-day highlight reel:

1. Filter by Red (Goal) markers - these are your must-include moments
2. Add Green (Action) moments for energy and pacing
3. Drop in Blue (Replay) moments with speed ramps
4. Use Yellow (Crowd) reactions as transitions between highlights

A 90-minute game that would take 3-4 hours to review and edit is done in under an hour.

## Multi-Camera Sports

If you're running multiple camera angles:

- The Companion auto-detects multiple cameras filming the same time range
- Each camera angle gets its own video track
- Markers are placed on the correct timecodes across all angles
- Your editor can cut between angles at any marker point

This is especially powerful for replay moments - switch to the close-up camera at the exact timecode of the Replay marker for maximum impact.
