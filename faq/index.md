# Frequently Asked Questions

## General

### What is Marker for Creators?
Marker for Creators is a two-part tool: an iOS app for setting markers during a shoot or review, and a desktop Companion app (Mac and Windows) that exports those markers directly into your NLE of choice.

### Is there a Windows version of the Companion app?
Yes. Marker for Creators Companion is available for both Mac and Windows.

---

## Features and Compatibility

### Does the Companion support multicam clips?
**Multicam support is coming.** Currently the Companion handles single-camera timelines. When you have a multicam project and import it, clips are treated as individual clips rather than a multicam group. Proper multicam support, where clips are recognized and exported as multicam groups, is actively being developed.

### Which NLEs are supported?
- Final Cut Pro X
- DaVinci Resolve
- Adobe Premiere Pro
- Generic CSV / spreadsheet

### Can I use the app without the Companion?
Yes. The iOS app works standalone for note-taking and marker management. The Companion is only needed when you want to export markers into your NLE.

## Pricing

### Do you offer a one-time payment?

We've thought about offering a one-time payment for Marker for Creators for a long time. After carefully considering it, we decided against it for a few important reasons.

A simple way to understand this is with a car example. There are two ways to get a car: you can buy it, or you can lease it.

When you buy a car, you own it, but over time it becomes outdated. 20 years later, you still have the same car, but 20 years older.

If you always want to have the newest model, you have to lease a car.

Software works in a similar way. Apps are not static products. They constantly evolve. New cameras come out, editing software changes, operating systems update, and workflows improve. The only, but huge difference to a car is: Marker for Creators needs to keep evolving to be work at all.

If we sold the app once for a large upfront price, it might generate a lot of money quickly, but that would not support long-term development. We've seen many indie apps with one-time payments that stop being updated after a year or two. Eventually they break, fall behind, or disappear entirely.

Marker for Creators is a long-term project. Our goal isn't to make quick money. Our goal is to help creators save time for many years.

A subscription allows us to:

- Continuously improve the app
- Provide long-term support
- Quickly adapt to updates from tools like DaVinci Resolve, Premiere Pro, and Final Cut Pro
- Build new features that make creators' workflows faster

This model benefits both sides. It gives us the stability to invest time and effort into development, and it gives you the confidence that the tool you learn today will still be supported and improved in the future.

We also try to keep the pricing fair. There is a free version that already helps hobby creators save time each month.

For professionals, Marker often saves multiple hours every week. That time can easily translate into more projects, faster edits, or more income. Most videographers save in one shoot what Marker for Creators costs a year. In that sense, the yearly subscription is designed to be a small fraction of the value the app creates over the full year.

This approach allows us to keep building Marker for Creators into the best tool it can be, year after year.
